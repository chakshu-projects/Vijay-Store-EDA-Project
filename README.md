# Vijay-Store-EDA-Project  
## 1) Project Overview  
  -  Performed end-to-end data analysis on an e-commerce sales dataset consisting of customer, order, product, and order item information.
  -  Cleaned and transformed raw data to improve data quality and consistency.
  -  Identified missing values, duplicate records, and inconsistent formatting across multiple datasets.
  -  Integrated multiple datasets using common keys such as customer_id, order_id, and product_id.
## 2) Dataset Information  
  -  **Customers Dataset**: Customer details, city information, and signup dates.
  -  **Orders Dataset**: Order details, order dates, and payment methods.  
  -  **Order Items Dataset**: Product quantities, pricing, and order-level information.
  -  **Products Dataset**: Product names, categories, and base prices.
## 3) Data Visualization  
  -  Created bar charts to compare product-wise and city-wise distributions.
  -  Used line charts to analyze sales trends over time.
  -  Generated correlation heatmaps to identify relationships between numerical variables.
  -  Developed summary visualizations for easier business insights.
## 4) Key Insights  
  -  The month January has contributed a major part in revenue and quantity sold.
  -  The product Dell Inspiron is the top performing among all the other products.
  -  Most part of the sales and quantity sold are take place in Delhi.
  -  The correlation graph help the business to understand the relation between the data.
## 5) Technologies Used  
  -  Python
  -  Pandas
  -  NumPy  
  -  Matplotlib
  -  Seaborn
  -  Jupyter Notebook
## 6) Conclusion  
The analysis revealed important business insights regarding sales performance, customer behaviour, product demand, and city-wise contributions. It transformed raw data into actionable business insights, providing a strong foundation for data-driven decision-making and future sales optimization strategies. Correlation analysis further showed a strong positive relationship between product quantity and revenue, indicating that higher sales volumes directly drive business growth
